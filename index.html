Coston Mission Blog

Year

2016

Service

Our family repository of everything about us.

Visit Site

Challenge

This was my first website to completely code from scratch. I had tried several templates from several different vendors (WordPress, SquareSpace, Wix, Zoho, Google Sites, and even the default GoDaddy site builder) but nothing could quite do everything I wanted to do. So I decided to switch to Blogger (because they give you full access to the files in xhtml, CSS, and javascript) and learn the front end web for myself.

Project

Being my personal blog I had complete liberty to style it however I wanted. I knew that my primary market was going to be reformed Christians who had probably been used to the navigation and readability of other major reformed Christian websites, so I tailored my style after some of theirs. The style of CostonMission is closely related to that of desiringGod.org and will probably undergo several style changes over the first few years of it's life.

Coston Mission Magazine

Year

2017

Service

Quarterly Online Magazine for the Coston Mission blog.

View Magazine

Challenge

This was my first out of the box idea. This project really set off my passion for digital design and web development. I wanted this site to look, feel, and be interacted with like a material magazine. I wanted to dimensions of the pages to be literal 8.5" by 11" ratio no matter the size of your window or device. This required my slideshow to have to be capable of showing 2 pages at a time on landscape screens but only 1 page at a time on portrait screens. I also had to learn all kinds of new techniques that I never even though about before, like how to control finger movements with javascript.

Project

For Coston Mission, I make a quarterly newsletter based off of the idea of "selling Jesus" like a Patagonia or Huckberry magazine with large pictures and small captions usually containing bible verses. This magazine intendeds to capture 1 singular theme based on whatever happened over those last few months with four sections scattered evenly throughout: Pray, Lean, Obey, and Work. Since coming to Christ in 2012 I have always loved the acronym PLOW to help remind me of the basic disciplines of the Christian walk, and now we get ot express them through our magazine.

Sadie Hope Blog

Year

2017

Service

Personal blog for a family friend.

Visit Site

Challenge

Blogs are everywhere. Almost everyone either has their own blog or has at least visited a blog. This means that everyone has a pre-formed idea of what to expect when they visit a blog. Sadie has unique and personal stories to share with the world so her blog needed to be simultaneously intuitive for the reader but very personalized. The result? A simple but robust, elegant but functional, and clean but information saturated personal blog.

Project

Sadie is a personal friend of Melissa's and this was one of my first projects for someone other than myself. Sadie was planning on using her Instagram account, @sadie_hope, as her primary marketing platform, so I wanted to integrate the instagram style into her blog as much as possible. I quickly recognized that she favored soft, low contrast colors so I included these in her blog along with slower-than-normal animations and interactions to give the user a blissful experience.
